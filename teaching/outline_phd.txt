The mathematical course of the PhD Programme in Economics is
structured in complementary modules. Different lecturers are assigned
to different modules. It is assumed that students already understand
the basic notion of calculus, as they are typically taught in the
undergraduate basic courses (see Prerequisite below). All PhD students
are required to attend all lectures. During the course the students
will be assigned home works in the form of exercises or elaborations
on specific topics. In order to follow the development of the
lectures, it is in general important that these assignments are duly
performed. Please report any problem to the teachers. If deemed
necessary, extra teaching hours, albeit in a limited amount, can be
organized. One or more copies of the reference textbook cited below
will be available at the Sant'Anna Library and students are not
required to buy a copy. In any case the essential material for the
different modules will be provided directly by the teachers. For any
issue or question regarding the course program and related activities
please contact the course coordinator (Giulio Bottazzi
<bottazzi@sssup.it>).

Detailed schedule
=================


Linear Algebra
--------------

:Lecturer: Settepanella
:Teaching material: [St; Se]
:Teaching period: first two weeks
:Load: 20 hours
:Topics:
	- linear spaces, linear operators
	- eigenspaces, diagonalization
	- Jordan form, S+N decomposition
	- complex space, Euclidean spaces, quadratic forms


Calculus
--------

:Lecturer: Barigozzi
:Teaching material: [Ru, chapters 3, 6 and 9]
:Teaching period: first two weeks
:Load: 20 hours
:Topics:
	- series
	- Riemann-Steltjes integrals 
	- functions in $\mathcal{R}^{N}$, continuity, differentiability 
	- global/local extrema  
	- curves in $\mathcal{R}^{N}$
	- integrals on curves


Probability
-----------

:Lecturer: Scalas
:Teaching material: [Du]
:Teaching period: third and fourth weeks
:Load: 20 hours
:Topics:
	- the meaning of probability 
	- a short history of probabilistic methods in economics and the social sciences 
	- Kolmogorov's axioms 
	- random variables 
	- important distributions 
	- introduction to stochastic processes 
	- Markov chains 
	- Poisson process 
	- Wiener process


Static and Dynamic Optimization
-------------------------------

:Lecturers: Bottazzi, Dindo
:Teaching material: [HS 3-9, 15; Ch]
:Teaching period: last three weeks
:Load: 40 hours
:Topics:
	- constrained optimization: Lagrange, Kuhn-Tucker conditions
	- differential equations: existence and uniqueness theorem
	- linear autonomous differential equations
	- general methods for solving differential equations
	- stability of solutions
	- dynamic optimization: Euler condition, optimal control
