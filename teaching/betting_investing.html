Introduction

In his '52 seminal paper Kelly proposes an original connection between information theory and betting strategies. His work revealed the possibility to investigate the problem of forecasting from a very general perspective. One of the topics of the following research has been to investigate the possibility to devise a strategy that outperforms a given set of competing strategies under minimal information about the nature and dynamics of the underline process.

Assignment

Your assignment is divided in two tasks. Each task starts from a specific paper which proposes a way of dealing with the problem of building, under certain conditions and in a specific sense, the "optimal" strategy or family of strategies. You are required to understand and implement, using a new database, the idea discussed in the paper. A comparative exercise of the proposed family of strategies with other alternatives will be highly appreciated.

Task 1: Betting

In 1974 Cover proposes a Universal Gambling Scheme that should, to a certain extent, perform the magic of beating any single strategy competitors propose. From football-data website you can download the time series of matches with the final outcome and odds proposed by different online gambling sites. You can see the odds proposed by the different bookmakers as forecasts on the outcome of the game. Investigate and compare their performance and, starting from the advice of these "experts", try to build a strategy, or "betting scheme", that leads to better asymptotic performances. Hint: Italian Serie A championship is consistently available from 2002-03.

Task2 : Investing

Not satisfied by having dominated the betting arena, in 1991 Cover moves to conquer the financial market with his Universal Portfolios. From yahoo!finance download daily and weekly time series of a portfolio of stocks. Compare the performance of different buy-and-hold and re-balancing strategies, possibly using out-of-sample analysis. Then try to implement Cover suggested strategy and discuss its performance. Hint: Pick a set of liquid, high capitalization, and diverse assets. A possible choice could be: Bank of America Corporation (BAC), General Electric Company (GE), Ford Motor Co. (F), Citigroup, Inc. (C), Pfizer Inc. (PFE), Hewlett-Packard Company (HPQ), JPMorgan Chase & Co. (JPM), AT&T, Inc. (T), Wells Fargo & Company (WFC) and J. C. Penney Company, Inc. (JCP).
